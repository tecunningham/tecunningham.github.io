I feel like Aristotle would recognize a lot of the pieces in the jigsaw that we're working on, but he would be surprised at where they've ended up.

The dolphin is next to the dog, not the fish. The far East is next to the far West. The evening star is next to the morning star, and the sun fits in among one set of stars, and the moon a different set of stars. The different races and sexes of humans probably are closer to one another than he would've expected, but they also end up near the apes and monkeys.

I think you could just show the jigsaw to Aristotle, how we've rearranged all the pieces that he already had available, and he'd recognize it as basically right because of the beautiful symmetric patterns that appear -- we've accumulated a lot of new pieces he didn't have, but they have helped us see patterns in the pieces that he already had available.

There are also collections of pieces which we have given up on trying to fit together. Aristotle and other early philosophers (and naive philosophers of today) try to slot together the different types of virtue, the different types of republic, the different types of relationship. These things now seem much less ordered -- there is less expectation that they'll all fit into a coherent whole.
