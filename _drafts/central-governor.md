Central governor theory on [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_governor).

Basic argument: in almost all cases your exercise is *not* up against the phsyical limits of your body: esp., when you stop running, it's not because your muscles have run out of oxygen or built up too much lactic acid. Instead there's a separate mechanism which intervenes to limit your use of the muscles: "fatigue is an emotion."

Evidence that people rarely have their foot to the floor:

* Perform better in the last stretch of the race (in 75% of world records, fastest lap was one of the last two laps - [Noakes in BMJ](http://bjsm.bmj.com/content/early/2008/04/18/bjsm.2008.046763.abstract); [correspondence](http://blogs.bmj.com/bjsm/the-end-spurt-does-not-require-a-subconscious-intelligent-system/) )
* Perform better with monetary incentives
* Perform better when you expect the exercise to end soon
* Perform better with various placebo manipulations
* Perform better with competition
* Perform better when you're misled about the time, or about past performance
