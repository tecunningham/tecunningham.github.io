title: Is Selfishness Instinctive or Deliberate?
---

There are a lot of recent papers arguing over whether cooperation is instinctive, or whether selfishness is instinctive, and cooperation only occurs when we suppress our selfish instincts. Dave Rand says “Most people think we are intuitively selfish, ... [but] our lab experiments show that making people rely more on intuition increases cooperation.” [1]

It sounds like a sensible question -- like whether the blood circulates or stays in the same place -- but here are some other questions:

* Is a preference for cats over dogs instinctive or deliberate?
* Is a preference for GM over Ford instinctive or deliberate?

Seems to me that the answer is that for some people, in some cases, the preference for one is instinctive, and that for some people in some cases it's the reverse. And if we run one study, looking at one type of decision, we don't learn much that we can generalize.

Regarding selfishness vs generosity: If my sister asks me for \$5 then my instinct is to say yes, which I might modify if I think about it more. If a stranger on the street asks me for \$5 then my instinct is to say no, which I might modify if I think about it more.
