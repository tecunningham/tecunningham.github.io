**OBSERVATIONS**

  (1) Regarding the things I shake my head at.

  (2) Regarding the things which are pretending to be something else. 
     
     (i) Sometimes we use this as a reason to dislike something: e.g. Bruno Munari: "a photo-frame like a pocket watch, scissors like a flamingo, a salt-cellar like a wheelbarrow with a spoon like a shovel ... these are certainly not objects produced by designers, for designers do not have such raging imaginations. They confine themselves to making candlesticks that look like candlesticks.”

     (ii) Yet there are many cases where it is baked deep into how a thing is. 
    
     (iii) Related to the etymology of words - many words were conceived in sin - their current meaning was originally a misunderstanding.
     
     (iv) Why not have a fucking salt-cellar like a wheelbarrow? 

     (v) A difference from the meaning of words: we mostly accept that language is both arbitrary and conventional - we need some sign, it doesn't matter which one, and there's little logical reason to prefer one over another. 

     (vi) Selective application of this principle: middle-class people who have positive associations about certain materials.


 (3) Reinterpreting something that is an end-in-itself as imperfect approximation of deeper ends:

  - the taste of coffee, whiskey, beer & wine, tobacco: just tastes which happen to co-occur with psychoactive drugs; not nice in themselves. (If there was caffeine in dirt, we'd be dirt connoiseurs, and we'd switch to decaffeinated dirt when we got older)
  - fruits & vegetables, in their current state, selectively bred for our use
  - clothes judged as an approach to some ideal
