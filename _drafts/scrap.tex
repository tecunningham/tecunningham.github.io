Setup:

* random variable $x$  and $s$, and let $\hat{x}=E[x|s]$, and make the following assumption:

$$
|E[\hat{x}|x]|>|x|
$$

And let's use the law of total variance:

$$
Var[A]=E[Var[A|B]]+Var[E[A|B]]
$$

First we show that the variance of $E[\hat{x}|x]$ must be greater than the variance of

$$
\begin{aligned}
Var[\hat{t}]=& Var[E[\hat{t}|t]]+E[Var[\hat{t}|t]] \\
Var[t]      =& E[Var[t|s]]+Var[\hat{t}]
\end{aligned}
$$

\textbf{Argument}

* The assumption implies that $Var[\hat{t}]>Var[t]$. But this contrdicts the law of total variance.
