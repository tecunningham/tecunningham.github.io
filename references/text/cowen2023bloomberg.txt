AI Won’t Supercharge the US Economy
===================================

The technology will probably provide only a small boost to economic growth, but it could still have a significant impact in the long run.

August 16, 2023 at 10:46 AM UTC


By [Tyler Cowen](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/authors/AS6n2t3d_iA/tyler-cowen)

Tyler Cowen is a Bloomberg Opinion columnist, a professor of economics at George Mason University and host of the Marginal Revolution blog.

It is a radical suggestion, no doubt, but some [analysts](https://www.openphilanthropy.org/research/could-advanced-ai-drive-explosive-economic-growth/) predict that AI might enable the US economy to achieve an annual growth rate of 30%. Even more dramatic accounts — though you should be wary of [biased authors](https://www.lettersfromai.com/the-ai-utopia/) — suggest that AI will, after reaching some critical mass of expertise, bring about a revolutionary utopia.

I am a believer in the power of current AI [trends](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-01-23/chatgpt-is-only-going-to-get-better-and-we-better-get-used-to-it?sref=htOHjx5Y). But a look at the way economies work argues for more moderate (but still substantial) estimates of AI’s impact. The most likely scenario is that economic growth will rise by a noticeable but not shocking amount.
